Frequently Asked Questions

Here are some of the frequently asked questions.

High-ticket offers for local businesses (e.g., dentists, gyms, contractors) where you charge €800–€2000 per site and focus on clear ROI like more bookings or leads, not just “nice design.” Profit comes from positioning as a solution provider, not a coder.

Manual outreach. Identify 50–100 local businesses with outdated websites, record short personalized Loom videos showing what’s wrong, and offer a simple improvement with a fixed price. Expect low response rates; volume matters more than perfection.

Execution frequency. The ones making money send offers daily, talk to real customers, adjust based on rejection, and track numbers. The others optimize systems, branding, or learning without selling.

Increase deal size, not workload. Instead of building 10 cheap sites, sell fewer premium offers with hosting, maintenance, SEO, or monthly retainers. Recurring revenue compounds.

If after consistent outreach (e.g., 100–200 quality attempts) there is no demand, no responses, or no willingness to pay. Lack of validation means the market does not value it — move to a more painful, urgent problem.
